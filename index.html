What is DMARC?

DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting & Conformance) is an email validation protocol that helps protect your domain from unauthorized use, such as email spoofing and phishing attacks. It ensures your emails are properly authenticated using SPF and DKIM, enhancing trust and improving email deliverability.

What is SPF?

SPF (Sender Policy Framework) is a security measure that specifies which mail servers are permitted to send email on behalf of your domain. SPF helps prevent spoofing by enabling recipient mail servers to verify that incoming mail is sent from an authorized source.

What is DKIM?

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) adds a digital signature to your emails, allowing the recipient&#39;s mail server to verify that the message truly originated from your domain and has not been altered during transmission. This significantly reduces the risk of email spoofing and enhances your sender reputation.

What is Hosted Email DNS (hosted SPF, DMARC, DKIM)?

Hosted Email DNS services simplify the management of your email authentication records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) by centrally managing these critical configurations. It ensures your email authentication remains secure, accurate, and optimized without the hassle of manual maintenance.

What is BIMI?

BIMI (Brand Indicators for Message Identification) is an email authentication standard that enables you to display your brand's logo next to authenticated emails in inboxes. It builds trust, increases brand recognition, and enhances the visual impact of your email communications.

What is TLS Encryption?

TLS (Transport Layer Security) encryption ensures emails are securely transmitted between mail servers, preventing unauthorized access or interception. Utilizing TLS helps protect sensitive information and maintains data privacy during email transmission.

What is Reverse DNS (rDNS)?

Reverse DNS (rDNS) is a method used to match an IP address with a domain name. Proper rDNS setup verifies email authenticity, improves deliverability, and is essential for building and maintaining a good sender reputation.

What is Email Authentication?

Email authentication verifies the legitimacy of email messages, confirming that they're sent from authorized sources. Common authentication methods include SPF, DKIM, and DMARC, which collectively help reduce spam, spoofing, and phishing risks.

What is List Hygiene?

List hygiene refers to regularly updating and cleaning your email lists by removing invalid, inactive, or risky addresses. Proper list hygiene helps maintain high deliverability rates, reduces bounce rates, and ensures your sender reputation remains strong.

What is Email Reputation?

Email reputation, or sender reputation, measures how trustworthy your email practices are perceived by mailbox providers. A strong reputation ensures your emails consistently land in recipients' inboxes, while a poor reputation may cause emails to be flagged as spam or blocked entirely.
